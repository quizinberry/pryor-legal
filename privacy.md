# Pryor Privacy Policy

**Last updated:** April 21, 2026

Pryor is a photo and video sharing app that lets people contribute to shared event albums in real time. This policy explains what we collect, how we use it, and your rights.

## Who we are

Pryor is operated by Joshua Quisenberry, based in Spokane, Washington, USA. If you have questions about this policy or your data, contact us at joshua.quisenberry@gmail.com.

## Information we collect

When you use Pryor, we collect the following:

**Account information** — your email address, display name, and the password you create (stored encrypted; we never see the plaintext).

**Profile information** — anything you choose to add to your profile, including bio, location (text, not GPS), and profile photo.

**Content** — photos and videos you capture using the Pryor in-app camera, along with the event they belong to and when they were taken.

**Social data** — your friendships, likes, event participation, and any friend, invite, or join requests you send or receive.

**Notification tokens** — a push notification token provided by Apple or Google so we can send you app notifications.

**Device permission choices** — whether you've granted permission for the camera, microphone (for video), and notifications.

**Reports and blocks** — records of content you report or users you block, used to improve safety on the app.

We do not collect your phone contacts, your device photo library, your precise GPS location, or your browsing history outside of Pryor.

## How we use your information

We use your information to:

- Provide the core Pryor experience — creating events, uploading photos and videos to shared albums, letting friends find and invite each other
- Send you notifications about friend requests, event invites, likes, and events starting soon
- Keep the app safe — reviewing reports, enforcing blocks, and complying with law
- Improve the app — diagnosing bugs and understanding how features are used (in aggregate only)
- Show advertising inside the app (see "Advertising" below)

## Who we share your information with

We share information only with the service providers we need to run Pryor:

- **Supabase** — our backend hosting provider. Your account info, photos, videos, and app data are stored on Supabase's servers in the United States.
- **Expo** — delivers our push notifications.
- **Google AdMob** — serves ads inside the app. AdMob may collect device identifiers and ad interaction data to show relevant ads.
- **Apple** — operates the App Store and TestFlight.

We do not sell your personal information to anyone. We do not share your personal information with data brokers. We do not share your data with advertisers beyond what AdMob needs to serve ads.

If Pryor is ever acquired or merges with another company, your data may be transferred to that company under this same policy.

We will disclose information if legally required — for example, in response to a subpoena, court order, or valid legal process.

## How photos, videos, and posts are shared

When you take a photo or video inside a Pryor event, it is automatically shared with all other participants of that event. Event hosts and participants can see, like, and download the content. When an event ends, the album is frozen but remains visible to participants.

You can delete your own photos and videos from any album at any time. Event hosts can remove any photo or video from their events. **However, deleted content is only removed from what users see in the app — Pryor retains uploaded media in backend storage indefinitely.** This is disclosed in our in-app terms and is a core part of how Pryor works.

## Advertising

Pryor is free to use and is supported by ads served by Google AdMob. AdMob may use device identifiers and interaction data to serve more relevant ads. You can reset your device's advertising identifier in your iOS or Android settings at any time to limit ad personalization.

We do not share your email, photos, videos, friends list, or event data with advertisers.

## Your rights and choices

**You can delete your account at any time** from within the app (Profile → Edit Profile → Delete Account). When you delete your account, your profile, friendships, likes, notifications, and blocks are permanently removed. Your photos and videos are hidden from the app but retained in backend storage, as disclosed above.

**You can edit your profile information** at any time from Edit Profile.

**You can block and report other users** at any time.

**You can disable push notifications** in your device settings.

Depending on where you live, you may have additional rights:

- **California (CCPA):** You can request to know what personal information we have about you, request that we delete it (subject to the photo retention note above), and request a copy. You can also opt out of the sale of your personal information — we do not sell personal information, but this right still exists. Email us to exercise these rights.
- **Europe and UK (GDPR):** You have the rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, and objection. Our legal basis for processing is your consent (by creating an account) and our legitimate interest in operating the app.

To exercise any right, email joshua.quisenberry@gmail.com with your request and the email associated with your account.

## Children

Pryor is not intended for users under 13 years old. We do not knowingly collect information from children under 13. If you believe a child under 13 has created an account, email joshua.quisenberry@gmail.com and we will delete it.

## Data security

We use industry-standard security practices, including encrypted connections (HTTPS) and encrypted password storage. No system is perfectly secure, but we work to protect your information.

## Data retention

- **Account data** (email, profile, friendships, likes) — retained until you delete your account
- **Photos and videos** — retained in backend storage indefinitely, even after display-layer deletion
- **Notifications** — retained for up to 90 days
- **Reports and blocks** — retained as long as the app operates, for safety review

## Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top reflects the most recent change. Material changes will be communicated in the app.
